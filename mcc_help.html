Medieval Calendar Calculator

To see a calendar for any year or month between 500 and 1582 CE, simply enter the year and select the month in the navigation bar at the top of this window. Feastdays are colour-coded according to their liturgical rank. If you pass your cursor over a coloured day, the name of the feast will appear on the status line at the foot of your screen.

This web site allows you to see the feastdays prescribed by different calendars or uses (e.g. different dioceses, religious orders, etc.). At the moment, the only calendars available are a generic one (extracted from Cheney's Handbook of Dates) and the medieval English Hereford Use. To see a different use, make a selection from the "Calendar" list at the upper right and click on "Load". Once the use is loaded, you may if you wish click on the button marked "Make this your default calendar". If you do, your browser will a receive a cookie containing the name of your selected use, and the next time you visit this site that use will be loaded automatically.

You may create your own customised calendars in addition to the ones I've provided. To do so, download the template and edit it to incorporate information about the feasts of your calendar. Full instructions are provided in the template. The template is given here as mcc_template.txt, which will appear in a new window of your browser (or right-click on the link and choose the "save as" option); you should save it, edit it with a text editor (the Windows Notepad will do), and then give it a unique name and change its extension from .txt to .htm. You can then load it using the "Custom" command at the end of the list of Calendar choices and typing the address of your file. If you like, you can post it on your web site and send me the URL; I will then add it to the list of available calendars. In this way I hope the range of available calendars will grow in a collaborative effort. If you don't have a web site, send me the file by email and I'll post it.

Please note (10 Jan. 2001): There was an error in the instructions in the template that has now been fixed. Where it said to enter the dates of immovable feasts in the form "month, day", it should have said "day, month". If you have tried to make a template and have given up in frustration, please try again! I apologize for the inconvenience.

If this site does not meet all your medieval calendrical needs, try Otfried Lieberknecht's excellent Calendar Utility, where you will find indictions and year-beginning styles of every description.
